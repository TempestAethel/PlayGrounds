# My Gaming Journey: Pokémon, Dynamons, and PokePath TD

I used to like Pokémon a lot when I was younger. I even owned the first two volumes of Pokémon, and I spent a big part of my childhood watching the series and enjoying it. It was a major part of my early gaming and entertainment experience.

I never really got into Pokémon GO when it became popular. The main reason was that it required a lot of movement, and at that time I wasn’t really allowed to carry my phone freely. Even though it was very popular, I didn’t feel ready or comfortable to play it. Even now, I still feel a bit unsure about it, but I do want to try it someday.

Earlier, I also played Dynamons. I remember catching almost all the creatures available in it at that time. However, none of that progress was saved, which was disappointing. That experience made me start disliking games that don’t properly save progress. Over time, it also made me begin documenting my gameplay or taking screenshots so I don’t lose progress or memories.

In general, I like tower defense (TD) games and similar strategy-based games. I also prefer games that properly save progress, since losing long sessions feels frustrating.

Recently—about a week ago from the time I’m writing this (18-05-2026)—I got a YouTube recommendation for gameplay of PokePath TD, which is how I first discovered it.
[PokePath TD Official Site](https://pokepath.gg/en/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

The game has a simple save system in its settings:

* Export lets you download your save file to your PC
* Import lets you load a save file from your PC back into the game

The save files are named like PokePathSave-01, PokePathSave-02, and so on within the "Saves" folder.

My PokePathSave-01 shows around 12 hours of in-game time. I actually played for almost an entire day and even left it running in the background, but it seems like the game didn’t fully register all the time played, which was a bit disappointing.
